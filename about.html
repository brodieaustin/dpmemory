About

Des Plaines Memory is an online archive of historical materials that tell a story about Des Plaines' past. By preserving everything from old photographs to telephone books in digital form, not only are we protecting these materials from desctruction but also make them easier to find and look at or read.

Continue reading to learn more about what you will find in Des Plaines Memory and who works on this exciting project.

What will you find here?

Des Plaines Memory contains a wide variety of material from different eras in Des Plaines History, going back to the mid-19th century. Much of the material that you see here comes from the Des Plaines History Center collections.

Collections

We organize the material in Des Plaines Memory into broad collections. We suggest that you start with the Des Plaines History collection.

Des Plaines History

Explore Des Plaines past with this collection of photographs, letters, postcards, and more. We feature items on several topics including Camp Pine, Brown's Department Store, Plew Homes, to name a few.

The Local Newspapers and Telephone Directories are only available in the library or the History Center.

Local Newspapers

Search through and read local newspapers from the 1930s to the present.

Telephone Directories

Find your ancestors, local business, and view vintage advertising in this collection of telephone directories.

How Des Plaines Memory began

Des Plaines Memory was first conceived during ILEAD U—Illinois Libraries Explore, Apply and Discover: The 21st Century Technology Tools Institute, a continuing education initiative developed by the Illinois State Library. The purpose of the ILEAD U program is to enable librarians to utilize new technologies to interact with their communities.

Both of our institutions wanted to make a searchable database that allowed for community interaction: leaving comments, tagging items and the ability to share content on social media.

From the outset we knew we wanted to develop our project in partnership with the Des Plaines History Center. The history center had a wealth of local history materials in its archives but public access was limited to in person visits. As a result of ILEAD U, the library had the technological training and equipment to create an online digital collection. Both of our institutions wanted to make a searchable database that allowed for community interaction: leaving comments, tagging items and the ability to share content on social media.

The scope of Des Plaines Memory has expanded from a local history collection to include old telephone directories and local newspapers.
